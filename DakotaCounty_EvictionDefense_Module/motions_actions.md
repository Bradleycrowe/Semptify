# Dakota County Eviction Defense – Motion & Action Templates

> Informational drafting aids for tenants/organizers. Not legal advice. Verify current Minnesota statutes and local court practices.
>
> Use these with the process flow in `process_flow.md` (avoid duplicating flow content here).

## Usage Overview
Each template below includes:
- Purpose: What problem it addresses
- When to File: Optimal timing relative to hearing/service
- Inputs Needed: Data or evidence to customize
- Optional Attachments: Suggested supporting documents
- Draft Template: Plain text motion with placeholders

### Placeholder Legend
| Placeholder | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| `{{CASE_NO}}` | Court-assigned case number |
| `{{TENANT_NAME}}` | Tenant full legal name |
| `{{LANDLORD_NAME}}` | Landlord name as listed in complaint |
| `{{HEARING_DATE}}` | Scheduled hearing date |
| `{{SERVICE_DATE}}` | Date tenant actually received service |
| `{{DEFECT_DETAILS}}` | Specific service defects (e.g., posted without prior attempts) |
| `{{UNSAFE_CONDITIONS}}` | Short list of habitability issues |
| `{{RENT_AMOUNT}}` | Monthly rent or arrears amount (if relevant) |
| `{{EVIDENCE_PENDING}}` | What evidence is still being gathered (e.g., inspection report) |
| `{{REASONS_EXPUNGE}}` | Grounds supporting expungement (dismissal, settlement, interests of justice) |
| `{{RETALIATION_FACTS}}` | Timeline of protected complaints vs. filing date |
| `{{HABITABILITY_FACTS}}` | Specific covenant of habitability violations |

## 1. Motion to Dismiss (Improper Service)
**Purpose:** Challenge defective service under Minn. Stat. § 504B.321.
**When to File:** As soon as defect identified; ideally before or at start of initial hearing.
**Inputs Needed:** Service method, timeline, defect specifics, affidavit irregularities.
**Optional Attachments:** Photos of posted notice, envelope, timeline log, witness statement.

```
STATE OF MINNESOTA
DISTRICT COURT
COUNTY OF DAKOTA

Case No. {{CASE_NO}}

Tenant’s Motion to Dismiss for Improper Service

Tenant {{TENANT_NAME}} respectfully moves this Court to dismiss the eviction action due to improper service of the summons and complaint pursuant to Minn. Stat. § 504B.321.

Grounds:
1. Service occurred on {{SERVICE_DATE}}, fewer than seven (7) days before the scheduled hearing on {{HEARING_DATE}}.
2. Defects: {{DEFECT_DETAILS}}.
3. The statutory requirements for personal, substituted, or posted + mailed service (after due diligence) were not satisfied.

WHEREFORE, Tenant requests dismissal of the action.

Dated: ___________

______________________________
{{TENANT_NAME}}
```

**Strategic Note:** Even if denied, preserves record for appeal and supports later expungement if procedural defects accumulate.

## 2. Motion to Continue (Need More Time)
**Purpose:** Obtain additional time to gather evidence or secure counsel (Minn. Stat. § 504B.341).
**When to File:** Before or at hearing if critical evidence pending.
**Inputs Needed:** Hearing date, specific pending items (inspection report, aid approval), reason counsel unavailable.
**Optional Attachments:** Scheduling confirmation, inspector appointment, aid application receipt.

```
STATE OF MINNESOTA
DISTRICT COURT
COUNTY OF DAKOTA

Case No. {{CASE_NO}}

Tenant’s Motion to Continue

Tenant {{TENANT_NAME}} respectfully requests a continuance of the hearing scheduled for {{HEARING_DATE}} to allow time to obtain and present essential evidence and/or secure legal representation.

Reasons:
1. Pending evidence: {{EVIDENCE_PENDING}}.
2. Additional time will not prejudice Landlord and is in the interest of justice.

WHEREFORE, Tenant requests the Court continue the hearing for a reasonable period.

Dated: ___________

______________________________
{{TENANT_NAME}}
```

**Strategic Note:** Identify a concrete, near-term deliverable (e.g., inspection report expected within 5 days) to strengthen request.

## 3. Motion for Rent Escrow (Habitability Issues)
**Purpose:** Redirect rent payments to court until repairs completed (Minn. Stat. § 504B.385) or reinforce existing habitability defense (§ 504B.161).
**When to File:** Once substantial habitability issues documented (photos, reports) and landlord notified.
**Inputs Needed:** Conditions list, notice dates, repair request timestamps.
**Optional Attachments:** Photos, city inspection notice, repair request emails, temperature/humidity logs.

```
STATE OF MINNESOTA
DISTRICT COURT
COUNTY OF DAKOTA

Case No. {{CASE_NO}}

Tenant’s Motion for Rent Escrow

Tenant {{TENANT_NAME}} moves the Court under Minn. Stat. § 504B.385 to order that future rent payments be deposited with the Court until Landlord corrects uninhabitable conditions, including: {{UNSAFE_CONDITIONS}}.

Supporting Facts:
1. Landlord notified on: (dates of each complaint).
2. Conditions substantially impair health/safety.
3. Repairs remain incomplete despite reasonable opportunity.

WHEREFORE, Tenant requests rent be escrowed until compliance.

Dated: ___________

______________________________
{{TENANT_NAME}}
```

**Strategic Note:** Escrow motion also creates structured record aiding later expungement or rent abatement claims.

## 4. Motion for Expungement (Clear Record)
**Purpose:** Remove eviction record to prevent housing barrier (Minn. Stat. § 484.014).
**When to File:** After dismissal, settlement favorable to tenant, or interests of justice conditions met.
**Inputs Needed:** Grounds: {{REASONS_EXPUNGE}}; case history timeline.
**Optional Attachments:** Dismissal order, settlement agreement, employment/housing hardship letters.

```
STATE OF MINNESOTA
DISTRICT COURT
COUNTY OF DAKOTA

Case No. {{CASE_NO}}

Tenant’s Motion for Expungement

Tenant {{TENANT_NAME}} moves the Court pursuant to Minn. Stat. § 484.014 to expunge the record of this eviction proceeding on the grounds of: {{REASONS_EXPUNGE}}.

Supporting Points:
1. Case procedural posture favors expungement.
2. Public interest outweighs need for record retention.
3. Continued record presence creates disproportionate hardship for Tenant.

WHEREFORE, Tenant requests an order expunging the eviction record.

Dated: ___________

______________________________
{{TENANT_NAME}}
```

**Strategic Note:** Frame hardship concretely (loss of prospective housing offers, employment screening issues).

## 5. Counterclaim (Retaliation / Habitability)
**Purpose:** Assert retaliation (Minn. Stat. § 504B.285) and habitability breach (§ 504B.161) to offset landlord claims or seek abatement.
**When to File:** With Answer or promptly thereafter.
**Inputs Needed:** Timeline of complaints vs. filing date; habitability issue list.
**Optional Attachments:** Complaint emails, inspection reports, repair tickets, medical impact notes.

```
STATE OF MINNESOTA
DISTRICT COURT
COUNTY OF DAKOTA

Case No. {{CASE_NO}}

Tenant’s Counterclaim

Tenant {{TENANT_NAME}} asserts the following counterclaims:

Count I – Retaliation (Minn. Stat. § 504B.285)
Landlord filed this eviction action after Tenant’s protected complaints regarding: {{RETALIATION_FACTS}}.

Count II – Breach of Habitability (Minn. Stat. § 504B.161)
Landlord failed to maintain the premises in a fit and habitable condition, including: {{HABITABILITY_FACTS}}.

WHEREFORE, Tenant seeks dismissal, rent abatement, and any other just relief.

Dated: ___________

______________________________
{{TENANT_NAME}}
```

**Strategic Note:** Link retaliation timeline explicitly: complaint date → landlord response → filing date proximity.

## Filing Logistics (Dakota County Remote Context)
- Submit via Minnesota Guide & File where available.
- If uploading PDFs, keep filenames descriptive (`Motion_Dismiss_ServiceDefect_CASE{{CASE_NO}}.pdf`).
- Retain local copies in Semptify vault with hash certificate.
- Track submission timestamps for appeal/expungement support.
