‚öñÔ∏è Your Fundamental Rights

Right to Habitable Housing

  • Safe, structurally sound building
  • Working plumbing, heat, electricity
  • No lead paint (pre-1978 units)
  • Protection from pests and mold

Right to Privacy

  • Landlord cannot enter without notice (typically 24-48 hours)
  • Limited entry for repairs, inspections, emergencies
  • Protection from harassment or intimidation

Right to Non-Discrimination

  • Cannot discriminate based on: race, color, national origin, religion, disability, family status, sex
  • Service animals are NOT pets (no restrictions)
  • Reasonable accommodations required for disabilities
üìå Pro Tip: Federal Fair Housing Act (FHA) protects against 7 protected classes. Document ALL discrimination with dates, times, witnesses.

üîí Legal Protections

Retaliation Protection

  • Cannot be evicted for reporting violations
  • Cannot be punished for joining tenant unions
  • Cannot face rent increases for exercising rights

Eviction Protections

  • Must receive proper written notice (period varies by state)
  • Right to cure lease violations
  • Cannot be "self-help" evicted (illegal lock-outs)
  • Court process required in most states

Repair Rights

  • "Repair and deduct" laws available in many states
  • Can withhold rent for uninhabitable conditions
  • Can request rent reduction for partial loss of use
‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Never pay reduced rent without documenting the reason in writing. Take photos and timestamps of all issues.

‚öîÔ∏è Legal Actions & Remedies

Before Court

  • Send formal demand letter (certified mail, return receipt)
  • Give landlord reasonable time to respond (typically 7-30 days)
  • File complaint with housing authority
  • Contact legal aid for free consultation

Legal Remedies Available

  • Compensatory Damages: Refunds, rent reductions, living costs
  • Punitive Damages: Additional payment for egregious violations
  • Attorney's Fees: Recover legal costs in many jurisdictions
  • Injunctive Relief: Court order to repair or comply

Small Claims Court

  • No lawyer needed - faster, cheaper than civil court
  • Usually limited to $5,000-$25,000 (varies by state)
  • Perfect for security deposit disputes, minor damages
