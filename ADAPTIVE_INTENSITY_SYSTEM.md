# ADAPTIVE INTENSITY & GOOD LANDLORD RECOGNITION

## PHILOSOPHY

**Not all situations require full legal intensity.**

- Good landlords who maintain properties and respond to issues deserve **recognition**, not complaints
- Being a landlord is an **investment in community**, the future, and fair monetary gain
- Bad tenants exist too - system recognizes **both sides**
- System scales response from **positive recognition** to **maximum pressure** based on situation

---

## INTENSITY LEVELS

### 1. POSITIVE ‚ú®
**When**: Everything going well, landlord is excellent
**Response**: Recognition mode - celebrate good landlords!
**Actions**:
- Leave positive review
- Generate recognition certificate
- Build positive relationship
- NO formal filing

**Message**: "Your landlord is doing a great job! Consider leaving a positive review."

---

### 2. COLLABORATIVE ü§ù
**When**: Minor issues, landlord generally responsive
**Response**: Friendly guidance - help resolve cooperatively
**Actions**:
- Friendly communication (call or text)
- Document issue with photos
- Give reasonable timeline
- Offer flexibility if needed

**Message**: "Start with friendly communication. Most responsive landlords will fix minor issues quickly."

**Template**: "Hi [Landlord], just wanted to let you know about [issue]. When you have a chance, could you take a look? Thanks!"

---

### 3. ASSERTIVE üìù
**When**: Issue persists OR landlord slow to respond
**Response**: Formal documentation - escalate appropriately
**Actions**:
- Send written notice (email or certified mail)
- Document everything (photos, videos, communications)
- Set clear deadline for repairs
- Mention legal obligations

**Message**: "Time for formal documentation. Send written notice with clear deadline."

**Template**: "Dear [Landlord], I am writing to formally notify you of [issue]. This affects [habitability/safety]. Please address within [X] days per [relevant law]."

---

### 4. ESCALATED ‚ö†Ô∏è
**When**: Serious issues OR landlord unresponsive
**Response**: Multi-venue filing - create pressure
**Actions**:
- File with code enforcement
- File with relevant agencies (HUD, health dept)
- Contact legal aid
- Consider rent escrow (with legal advice)
- Document EVERYTHING

**Message**: "Formal complaints needed. File with multiple agencies and document everything."

---

### 5. MAXIMUM üö®
**When**: Critical safety issues OR hostile landlord
**Response**: All venues + maximum pressure
**Actions**:
- File with ALL applicable venues immediately
- Emergency inspections (code enforcement, health dept)
- Legal aid immediately
- Consider media/public pressure
- Emergency court action if needed

**Message**: "URGENT: Critical safety issue or illegal activity. File everywhere immediately and seek legal help."

## HOW INTENSITY IS DETERMINED

```
INTENSITY = f(Issue Severity, Landlord Responsiveness, Time Elapsed, History)

ISSUE SEVERITY:
- Good (0): No issues or resolved
- Minor (1): Small issues, not urgent
- Moderate (2): Real issues, needs attention
- Serious (3): Health/safety concerns
- Critical (4): Immediate danger, illegal activity

LANDLORD RESPONSIVENESS:
- Excellent (-1): Proactive, fixes before asked
- Good (0): Responsive, reasonable timeline
- Fair (0): Eventually responds, needs reminders
- Poor (+1): Slow, makes excuses
- Hostile (+2): Ignores, retaliates, threatens

TIME ELAPSED:
- < 7 days: +0
- 7-14 days: +0.5
- 14-30 days: +1
- > 30 days: +2

LANDLORD HISTORY:
- Excellent track record (avg 4.5+ stars): -1
- Poor track record (avg <2 stars): +1

FINAL SCORE ‚Üí INTENSITY LEVEL:
0 = POSITIVE
1 = COLLABORATIVE
2 = ASSERTIVE
3 = ESCALATED
4 = MAXIMUM
```

## FAIRNESS: BAD TENANTS EXIST TOO

### System Recognizes BOTH Sides

**Good Landlords**:
- Deserve recognition certificates
- Lower intensity recommendations
- Benefit of doubt for minor issues
- Community asset acknowledgment

**Bad Landlords**:
- Higher intensity recommendations
- Escalate faster
- More venues filed with
- Public accountability

**Good Tenants**:
- Reliable, pay on time
- Respectful of property
- Communicate issues properly
- Follow lease terms

**Bad Tenants**:
- Late rent payments
- Property damage
- Lease violations
- Noise complaints

**Landlord Investment Perspective**:
- Landlords provide housing (essential service)
- Property maintenance costs money
- Fair rent = fair return on investment
- Good landlords invest in community future

### System Balance

Not all tenants are angels. Not all landlords are bad.

System provides:
- **Tenant perspective**: Filing venues, intensity scaling, outcome tracking
- **Landlord recognition**: Certificates for good landlords, reputation system
- **Fairness**: Both sides can report issues, both sides get recognition
