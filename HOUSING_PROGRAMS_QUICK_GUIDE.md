## üí∞ Can't Pay Rent?

### Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher
- **What**: Monthly help with rent
- **Who**: Low-income families (50% of area median)
- **How**: Apply through local Public Housing Authority
- **Note**: Long waitlists (2+ years) - apply NOW
- **Find**: hud.gov ‚Üí "Find your local PHA"

### Emergency Rental Assistance (ERAP)
- **What**: Up to 18 months rent/utility help
- **Who**: 80% area median, COVID hardship
- **Note**: Check if still available in your area
- **Find**: Google "[Your City] ERAP"

### Salvation Army / Catholic Charities
- **What**: Emergency rent/utility assistance
- **How**: Visit or call local office
- **Find**: Google "Salvation Army [your city]"

## ‚ö° Utility Shutoff Notice?

### LIHEAP (Priority)
- **What**: Emergency utility help (18-48 hours)
- **Who**: Low income (typically 150% poverty line)
- **HOW**: Say "I have a shutoff notice" immediately
- **Find**: Google "[Your State] LIHEAP"
- **Phone**: Call your state's LIHEAP office

### Local Utility Programs
- Many utilities have hardship programs
- Call utility company and ask for "customer assistance"

## ‚ùì Common Questions

**Q: I'm over the income limit. Any help available?**
A: Maybe! Some programs use area median income (higher than poverty line). Also check nonprofit programs (many have no income limits for one-time emergency help).

**Q: I'm undocumented. Can I get help?**
A: Some programs require citizenship, but many nonprofits don't. Call 211 - they know which programs are available to everyone.

**Q: How long does it take?**
A: Varies wildly:
- Emergency LIHEAP: 18-48 hours
- Nonprofit emergency funds: 1-7 days
- Section 8: 2+ YEARS (get on waitlist NOW)
- Legal aid: Immediate for emergencies

**Q: Will this affect my immigration status?**
A: Most programs are NOT considered "public charge" under immigration law, but check with an immigration attorney if concerned. Emergency assistance is typically safe.

**Q: Do I have to pay this back?**
A: NO! These are grants/vouchers, not loans. You don't pay them back.

**Q: My landlord says they won't accept Section 8. Is that legal?**
A: Depends on your state/city. Some ban "source of income discrimination." Check with HUD counseling or legal aid.
