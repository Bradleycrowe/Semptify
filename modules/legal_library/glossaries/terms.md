# Legal & Housing Terms Glossary

Plain-language definitions for common terms tenants encounter in housing law and rights documents.

## General Legal Terms

**Affidavit**: A written statement made under oath that can be used as evidence in court.

**Complaint**: The formal document filed with a court to start a lawsuit, outlining the plaintiff's claims.

**Defendant**: The person or entity being sued or charged in a legal case.

**Plaintiff**: The person or entity who brings a lawsuit against another.

**Pro Se**: Representing yourself in court without an attorney (Latin: "for oneself").

**Service of Process**: Official delivery of legal documents notifying someone of a court case.

**Statute of Limitations**: The deadline for filing a lawsuit after an incident occurs.

## Housing & Tenant Rights

**Habitability**: Legal requirement that rental property must be safe and livable (working heat, water, no severe hazards).

**Lease**: A contract between landlord and tenant specifying rent, duration, and obligations.

**Month-to-Month Tenancy**: A rental arrangement that continues monthly unless either party gives notice to end it.

**Notice to Vacate**: Formal notification from landlord requiring tenant to leave by a specified date.

**Retaliatory Eviction**: Illegal eviction in response to tenant exercising legal rights (e.g., reporting code violations).

**Security Deposit**: Money paid upfront by tenant, held by landlord to cover potential damages or unpaid rent.

**Withholding Rent**: Legal tactic in some jurisdictions where tenants hold rent in escrow when landlord fails to maintain habitability.

## Minnesota-Specific

**Redemption Period**: In Minnesota, time after eviction judgment when tenant can pay back rent to keep housing (often 7 days).

**Triple Damages**: Minnesota law allows tenants to recover 3x their security deposit if landlord wrongfully withholds it.

## Funding & Assistance

**Emergency Rental Assistance (ERA)**: Federal/state programs providing funds for back rent and utilities.

**Section 8 (Housing Choice Voucher)**: Federal subsidy program helping low-income tenants pay rent.
